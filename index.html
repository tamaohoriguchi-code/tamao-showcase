The AI PMO Skill Pack

Six PMO-workflow tools that encode senior project-management judgment as governed, reusable AI capabilities.

I'm a program and project manager with twenty years in enterprise delivery — luxury retail, multi-country platform builds, and offshore team leadership — now working at the layer that scopes, governs, and directs AI initiatives rather than the one that codes them. Each skill in this pack takes a judgment a senior PM makes under pressure — reading a status report, running a pre-mortem, translating for a stakeholder, structuring AI accountability, maintaining a RAID log, capturing a decision — and encodes it as an auditable tool with a human approval gate. The agent surfaces; the PM owns.

Skill 04

AI Governance RACI Generator

AI-specific
Problem
AI projects fail to name four governance roles again and again — Data Steward, Responsible AI, Model Risk / Validation Owner, and Operational Model Owner — not because teams are careless, but because no structured process checks for them at initiation. It's an accountability design failure, and it's usually discovered at the gate, with deployment weeks away and no one to provide independent validation.
Judgment encoded
The tool classifies the AI initiative by type, then generates an accountability structure calibrated to that type's failure modes — an automated decision-making system and a reporting dashboard get materially different governance. Its anchor rule: an absent governance role is never proxied. A missing Accountable owner shows as a visibly blank cell with a footnote citing the rule, never a convenient stand-in — because a RACI that only looks complete gives the team false confidence that validation is covered. Gaps are mapped to their NIST AI RMF function, with gate conditions that can't be papered over.
Outcome
Governance gaps made visible and actionable before the first gate closes. The PM knows not just that a role is absent, but which decisions are blocked, which gates can't close, and exactly what each incoming role will own from day one.
Skill 01

Status Report Synthesis

Universal
Problem
Sponsors make scope, budget, and timeline calls off status reports — yet RAG language, escalation criteria, and self-reported status get applied inconsistently from report to report. That's a governance risk, not a formatting one.
Judgment encoded
The tool computes project status from the raw data independently of the PM's self-report, then flags the gap between the two — its sandbagging check. It separates risks from issues from blockers, routes escalations and team wins to different sections, and checks the RAID health signal against the computed status. Seven judgment calls in all.
Outcome
Every status assessment is traceable to a named rule and threshold — auditable governance rather than intuition — and the PM reviews and approves before anything is distributed.
Skill 02

Pre-Mortem Risk Scan

Universal
Problem
The signals before a project fails are usually already there — a vendor gone quiet, an unsigned baseline, a disengaged sponsor. What's missing is a repeatable way to surface them before they become crises, instead of relying on individual memory and point-in-time assessments that miss compound risk.
Judgment encoded
The tool matches project signals against a 103-pattern failure library, surfaces the five patterns that actually block a project in a STOP-AND-ADDRESS block before the main report, and tracks unresolved patterns across sprints — so a risk that was manageable at Sprint 4 and still open at Sprint 8 is flagged as compounding, with the prior evidence cited.
Outcome
Forward-looking risk coverage that doesn't depend on how much time the PM had to prepare — and the most important finding is surfaced first, not buried at row eleven of a table.
Skill 03

Stakeholder Translation

Universal
Problem
One project position has to reach the executive, the customer, and the team — each needing a different version to act on it correctly. In practice, PMs make that calibration informally, under pressure, from habit, and sometimes soften difficult news in ways that create false stability.
Judgment encoded
The tool calibrates abstraction and confidence language per audience while enforcing a floor: no translation may contradict the official RAG status, and certain facts — security events, timeline slips affecting customer commitments, unowned blockers — survive every translation unchanged. On a contradiction in the input, it stops and waits for the PM rather than resolving it on its own.
Outcome
Governed register calibration that preserves the truth under pressure to soften it, with every customer-facing output gated on a conscious PM decision.
Skill 05

RAID Log Intelligence

Universal
Problem
A RAID log that isn't actively maintained stops being a risk tool. Risks that have already materialized sit in the Risk column because no one reclassified them; priorities drift from the context they were logged in; and the status report no longer matches the project's real posture.
Judgment encoded
The tool re-categorizes every item from its characteristics rather than its original label, graduates a Risk to an Issue when its trigger has fired — with an explicit Watch stage for the "approaching but not yet materialized" gap the framework doesn't define — reassesses priority against current milestone proximity, and treats severity and urgency as separate dimensions in escalation.
Outcome
A risk posture assessed against current project context, not log-entry context — with a graduated Issue surfaced before the status report is written, not discovered at the post-mortem.
Skill 06

Decision Log with Why-Capture

Universal
Problem
A decision log that records what was decided but not why is a list of outcomes whose reasoning has left the building. Five sprints on, a reversal forces the team to re-litigate the whole evaluation under schedule pressure — the rationale existed, it was just never captured, and deferred capture is lost capture.
Judgment encoded
The tool tests whether a discussion has actually hardened into a ratified decision worth capturing, records the why at a depth calibrated to the decision's type and weight, flags the load-bearing assumptions a decision rests on, preserves the original record when a decision is reversed — with the sunk costs stated — and never fills its own authority gaps, holding an unratified decision at Pending Review instead.
Outcome
A defensible record of what was decided, why, by whom, and on what assumptions — captured while it's still recoverable, not reconstructed after the rationale has gone cold.
